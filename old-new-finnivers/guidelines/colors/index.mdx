- Use colors to give elements attention from the user - this helps the user finish their task.
- Don't have many colors that will compete for attention.
- Do not use color with negative assosiation to communicate something positive, or the other way around.
- Follow our guidelines for color combinations to make sure your design is accessible for those with poor sight. We are required by the Norwegian Law to be accessible.

## Background usage

Milk is our main background color.

<Panel type="info">
  <strong>Highlight, group, or separate information:</strong> Use Ice when you
  have information that should be highlighted, grouped, or separated slightly
  from the content.
</Panel>
<Panel type="tips">
  <strong>Tip box: </strong>Tip is information we want to emphasize to the user.
</Panel>
<Panel type="new">
  <strong>New functionality: </strong>If we release new functionality, it may
  need a little extra attention. These areas will therefore have color Mint with
  2px border with the color Pea. More about styling and guidelines to new
  functionality can be found in the "new functionality"-tab on your left hand
  side menu.
</Panel>
<Panel type="warning">
  <strong>Warning</strong> (Banana): If there is something we want make user
  aware of, something is on hold, or something may affect the flow. Broadcasts
  are an example.
</Panel>
<Panel type="error">
  <strong>Error</strong> (Salmon): When things go wrong, or something happens
  that has a negative impact on the user.
</Panel>
<Panel type="success">
  <strong>Success</strong> (Mint): When all has gone well, and the action is
  positive. It differs from the design of the "new functionality" in that it
  does not have the frame.
</Panel>
